## AWS Billing and Pricing

- AWS Billing helps you track and manage the cost of the AWS services you use.
- You can view your usage, download invoices, set budgets, and monitor spending from the **Billing Dashboard**.

---

## AWS Pricing Model

- AWS follows a **Pay-as-You-Go** pricing model.
- You pay only for the resources you use.

**Example:**

* Run an EC2 instance for 5 hours → Pay for 5 hours (or seconds, depending on the instance type).
* Store 50 GB in S3 → Pay only for 50 GB of storage.

---

## AWS Free Tier

- AWS offers a **Free Tier** for new users to explore services at no cost within certain limits.
Examples:

* 750 hours/month of eligible EC2 usage
* 5 GB of S3 storage
* 25 GB of DynamoDB storage

> If you exceed the Free Tier limits, normal AWS charges apply.

---

## Pricing Models

### 1. On-Demand

* No long-term commitment
* Pay only for what you use
* Best for short-term or unpredictable workloads

---

### 2. Reserved Instances (RI)

* Commit for 1 or 3 years
* Lower cost than On-Demand
* Best for steady workloads

---

### 3. Spot Instances

* Use AWS's unused EC2 capacity
* Can be up to 90% cheaper
* AWS can interrupt the instance at any time

Best for:

* Testing
* Batch processing
* Big data workloads

---

### 4. Savings Plans

* Commit to a consistent amount of usage
* Flexible across eligible AWS services
* Lower cost than On-Demand

---

## Billing Dashboard

The Billing Dashboard allows you to:

* View current charges
* Check monthly bills
* Download invoices
* Monitor service-wise costs
* Track Free Tier usage

---

## AWS Cost Management Tools

### AWS Budgets

Set a spending limit and receive alerts when your costs exceed the budget.

**Example:**

- Monthly budget: **$20**
- If usage reaches **80%**, AWS can send an email notification.

---

### Cost Explorer

Helps analyze AWS spending over time.

You can:

* View daily or monthly costs
* Identify expensive services
* Forecast future spending

---

### Cost and Usage Report (CUR)

Provides detailed billing and usage data for advanced cost analysis and reporting.

---

## Tips to Reduce AWS Costs

* Stop unused EC2 instances.
* Delete unused EBS volumes and snapshots.
* Remove unused Elastic IPs.
* Use S3 Lifecycle policies for old data.
* Monitor spending with AWS Budgets.
* Choose the correct pricing model.
* Delete unused resources.

---

## Key Points

* AWS uses a **Pay-as-You-Go** pricing model.
* The **Free Tier** lets new users try many services for free within usage limits.
* Common pricing options are **On-Demand**, **Reserved Instances**, **Spot Instances**, and **Savings Plans**.
* Use **Billing Dashboard**, **Cost Explorer**, and **AWS Budgets** to monitor costs.
